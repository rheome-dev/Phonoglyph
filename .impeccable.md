## Design Context

### Users
Solo producers primarily, with labels and event promoters as secondary. They create audio-reactive visual content for social media (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube) using a drag-and-drop stem-to-visual mapping system. Desktop browser only. They're working at a studio desk, likely evenings, building release-ready clips quickly.

### Brand Personality
**Stone, bone, and amber.** Not silicon and chrome. The interface feels like it was carved, not generated — organic textures, warm weight, subtle imperfection. A bit magical (the audio-to-visual mapping should feel like a spell being cast), a bit retro-futuristic (thinking: CRT amber monitors, analog studio hardware, not Tron).

**Three-word voice:** Organic, premium, quietly magical.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm stone materiality. Neither cold tech-gray nor neon-AI-gradient. Think: a high-end analog synthesizer panel, or a ceramic sculpture that glows faintly when sound passes through it. Not "dark mode with purple glows" — that is the enemy.

**References:** Analog studio hardware (Moog, Elektron), aged amber CRT phosphors, ceramic/terracotta craft objects, Japanese mingei (folk craft) sensibility.

**Anti-references:** Neon SaaS dashboards, crypto landing pages, Figma template UIs, After Effects defaults, anything gradient-text-heavy.

### Theme
Both light and dark — the user chooses. But the warmth carries across both. Stone-900 in dark, warm cream in light.

### Color Direction
Abandon purple. The single most recognizable AI-slop color signal.
- **Primary accent:** Blue-green / verdigris — like aged copper or deep jade. Cool but organic, not tech-cold.
- **Secondary:** Sage or dusty teal — muted, natural, complementary
- **Neutrals:** Warm stone grays (stone-50 through stone-900) — tint everything very slightly warm, never cool-gray
- No neon, no cool grays, no blue-tinted surfaces

### Typography
- **Display:** Something with organic character — slightly irregular, calligraphic, or editorial. Cormorant or Syne at heavy weight. Not Fraunces (overused reflex), not a geometric sans (too tech).
- **UI/Body:** Clean, warm, readable. Plus Jakarta Sans or Instrument Sans. Not Inter.
- **Mono:** VT323 (already in use) — keep it for technical readouts. It has the right retro-terminal warmth.
- Scale: 5-step, high contrast between sizes. Tighter leading in dense UI panels.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over cool precision.** Every color choice check: does this feel like stone, bone, or amber? If it feels like a tech startup, reject it.
2. **Hand-crafted over generated.** Subtle imperfections are a feature. Rounded corners slightly irregular. Spacing that breathes. Nothing perfectly uniform.
3. **Magical moments, not constant sparkle.** Motion should feel like analog mechanisms engaging — smooth, weighted, deliberate. Not bouncy or elastic.
4. **Premium restraint.** The interface earns attention through material quality, not visual noise. No glassmorphism everywhere. Use it intentionally, not decoratively.
5. **Audio-reactive as the core miracle.** The mapping UI is where the magic lives — it should feel like casting a spell, not configuring a DAW.
